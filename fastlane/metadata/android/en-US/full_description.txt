OpenCode Mobile is a free, open-source (MIT) Android client for the opencode AI coding agent (sst/opencode). Connect to your own self-hosted opencode server and drive AI-powered coding sessions from your phone — no proprietary backend, no mandatory accounts, no tracking by default.

KEY FEATURES

• Multiple connection types — local network, Cloudflare Tunnel, ngrok, or Tailscale to reach any self-hosted opencode instance
• Real-time streaming chat — watch your AI agent think and respond live
• Inline diff viewer — see exactly what code changes the agent proposes before you accept
• Tool-call approval — review and approve agent actions before they run on your code
• Session management — browse, create, and resume coding sessions
• Biometric unlock — OS-level fingerprint / PIN to keep your sessions private
• Secure on-device credential storage — connection secrets are kept in the Android Keystore via expo-secure-store

WHO IT'S FOR

• Developers who run opencode (sst/opencode) on their workstation or a server
• Engineers who want to check in on long-running coding sessions away from their desk
• Privacy-conscious developers who want a mobile coding companion with zero vendor lock-in

REQUIREMENTS

• Your own opencode server, started with `opencode serve`
• A way to reach it from your phone — local network, or a tunnel such as Tailscale, Cloudflare Tunnel, or ngrok
• Your own AI provider keys, configured on your server — they stay on your server and never touch this app

OPEN SOURCE + PRIVACY BY DEFAULT

OpenCode Mobile is MIT licensed. Source code, issue tracker, and community discussion live at:
https://github.com/dzianisv/opencode-mobile

There are no ads. Crash reporting (Sentry) is opt-in and OFF by default. Your code, prompts, and AI responses go directly between your phone and your own server — the app sends nothing elsewhere unless you explicitly enable crash reporting in Settings → Privacy.

SELF-HOSTED FIRST

The app requires you to bring your own opencode server. This is a feature, not a limitation — your AI traffic goes directly to your backend, which can connect to Claude, GPT, Gemini, local models, or any opencode-compatible endpoint. No middleman, no required subscription.

ANTI-FEATURES DISCLOSURE

NonFreeNet: The opencode server you connect to may in turn connect to proprietary AI services (for example OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google). The app itself contains no proprietary network code and does not require any specific provider.
